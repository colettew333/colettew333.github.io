Color

Additive

Additive color mixing begins with black and ends with white; as more color is added, the result is lighter and tends to white.

Subtractive

Subtractive color mixing means that one begins with white and ends with black; as one adds color, the result gets darker and tends to black.

Theory of Color

Three basic categories of color theory: The color wheel, color harmony, and the context of how colors are used. Color theories create a logical structure for color.

Primary - any of a group of colors from which all other colors can be obtained by mixing.

Secondary - cyan, magenta and yellow

Tertiary - combinations of primary and secondary colours.

Color Models

Used to classify colors and to qualify them according to such attributes as hue, saturation, chroma, lightness, or brightness

Analogous

colors that sit next to each other on the color wheel

Monochromatic

all the colors (tints, tones, and shades) of a single hue

Triadic

One color to dominate, a second to support and the third color is used as an accent. A triadic color scheme uses colors that are evenly spaced around the color wheel

Complementary

pairs of colors which, when combined, cancel each other out

Green - cleanliness, nature, relaxation, safety

Blue - order, control, technology, depth
